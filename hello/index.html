Frequently asked questions

How did this ring come about?
The ring solves multiple problems but the initial core problem was that of security and access control. The ring is an ideal replacement for house keys. John McLear is the inventor of an NFC Ring, development took over a year as he wanted a ring with was relatively stealthy and this posed various challenges. He also wanted the ring to be metal, plastic rings are really uncomfy. The NFC Ring can be worn in all conditions(see ntag203 spec sheet), all year round.
Which digital door locks work with the ring?
Any NFC door lock will work however be mindful of cheep imitation door locks that don't have genuine NFC support. We recommend the Samsung SHS-2421 as a good sub 100$ digital door lock that is easy to fit.
What operating systems will be supported at Launch?
Android.
What's the deal with printing my own ring out at home??
We provide a size 9.5 STL file for 3d printing your own NFC Ring, we will ship you some of our inlays. We're doing this to show our love for the hacker community and to get as many people with access to prototyping and developing as quickly and as affordably as possible.
What operating systems will be supported after Launch?
We will set kickstarter targets where people can vote to get their OS supported. The app is developed with Apache Cordova(PhoneGap) so supporting additional devices should be relatively trivial.
What other hardware devices can interact with the ring?
Raspberry Pi and Arduino both have NFC modules available that work great with the ring.
What's the status with unlocking my phone with the ring?
You can use third party software to achieve this however third party software is somewhat limited. We will be setting Kickstarter targets for achieving this natively in Android
Why chose open source and what does it mean for the project?
A ring should be both personal and community focused, because sharing is a big part of the core functionality we needed a way to easily support third party platforms, open source allows this. I'm technical, show me the Organization on github
Why put two inlays in the ring?
For privacy. One inlay is private, one inlay is public. Different hand gestures mean different information is shared. The initial ring prototypes had one inlay and this meant everytime you shared some public information such as a twitter link you are sharing some private information such as a code for a digital door lock.
What is the read distance?
~<1mm. This is a feature not a bug. You can't have any security if the inlays can be read from a distance. We basically say that this is touch technology.
How many rings currently exist?
50 wood inlays, 1 completely clear, 100 or so prototypes (mostly 3d printed)
What can the ring be used for?
Unlocking doors, Unlocking mobile phones, sharing URLS, sharing information. John uses his for his digital door locks, to share contact information and as a replacement for his car ignition button
Who is behind the product?
John McLear, the founder of Primary Technology and the Etherpad Foundation and Matt Mullenweg, the founder of Wordpress(Automattik) and various other projects.
What will the price be at launch?
Ranging from 20$ to 600$
What size rings are available?
Ring sizes 7 to 15
What is the storage capacity of each inlay in the ring?
144 bytes
Is the inlay actual NFC and not an imitation?
Yes, we use the ntag203 chip
Can I purchase the 6mm inlay directly?
Minimum order quantity of 100000 units
What is the width of the ring?
Roughly 7mm
Can the ring be used to make payments?
Not yet and this isn't on our roadmap
Is the data stored on the ring encrypted or secured?
Your physical security is the security, there is no encryption. It is very similar to a door key.
Can I purchase rings in bulk for my exhibition/company or so?
After Kickstarter we will be taking orders of bulk purchases of the ring
